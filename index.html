Biography

René Descartes was born on March 31, 1596, in La Haye en Touraine, France. He is often referred to as the "father of modern philosophy" due to his significant contributions to the field. Descartes is best known for his statement "Cogito, ergo sum" ("I think, therefore I am"), which emphasizes the importance of doubt and questioning in the pursuit of knowledge.
